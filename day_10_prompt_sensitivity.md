# Day 10: Prompt Sensitivity & Temperature

### **Objective**
To understand "Temperature" (Randomness vs. Determinism) and how to adjust your language to get either "Creative" or "Factual" results.

### **The Concept**
* **Low Temperature (0.0 - 0.3):** The "Robot" mode. Best for facts, code, and technical troubleshooting. It picks the most likely next word, making it predictable and stable.
* **High Temperature (0.7 - 1.0+):** The "Artist" mode. Best for brainstorming, storytelling, and creative writing. It takes risks and picks less likely words, leading to "creativity."

### **The "Handshake" Calibration**
When you are coding for GitHub or updating your resume, you want **low sensitivity**. When you are writing custom dinosaur stories for Kaiden, you want **high sensitivity**.

### **Tactical Phrases**
* **For Precision:** "Provide a literal, concise answer. Do not use flowery language. Stick strictly to the provided data."
* **For Creativity:** "Be expressive, use metaphors, and explore unconventional ideas. Give me 5 'outside-the-box' variations."

### **Implementation Task**
1. Ask the AI to define "AI Fluency" twice. 
2. First, tell it to be "strictly technical and academic." 
3. Second, tell it to be "poetic and geared toward an 11-year-old." 
4. Compare how the "vibe" of the answer changes based on your constraints.
